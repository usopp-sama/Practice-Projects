Life

Early Life and Career

  • November 3, 1928: Tezuka Osamu is born in Toyonaka, Osaka.
  • Childhood: Hails from a prosperous, educated family with a military history.
  • Influence: Mother inspires creativity; Takarazuka theater shapes his art style.
  • Disney Inspiration: Becomes a Disney movie enthusiast and starts drawing comics.
  • Pen Name: Adopts the pen name "Osamushi" inspired by a ground beetle.
  • Manga Skills: Develops manga skills during school and creates amateur works.
  • World War II: Works in a factory while continuing to write manga.

Career Milestones

  • 1946: Publishes first professional work, "Diary of Ma-chan."
  • 1947: Collaborates on "New Treasure Island," beginning the golden age of manga.
  • 1950: Achieves breakthrough success with "Kimba the White Lion."
  • 1951: Graduates from medical school, introduces "Astro Boy" (Ambassador Atom).
  • Joins the Tokyo Children Manga Association.

Astro Boy and Beyond (1952-1960)

  • 1952: Astro Boy (Tetsuwan Atom) gains immense popularity.
  • 1953: Publishes "Princess Knight," an influential shōjo manga.
  • 1954: Starts "Phoenix," a significant life's work.

Tezuka's innovative storytelling and the popularity of Astro Boy solidified his position as a pioneering figure in manga and anime. His creations have left a lasting impact on Japanese pop culture and the global entertainment industry.

Shift to Gekiga Manga (1967-1980s)

  • 1967: Launches the magazine COM, shifts to a more realistic art style.
  • Dark Themes: Manga targets an adult audience with explicit violence and crime.
  • 1980s: Narrative tone becomes milder in later works.

Later Life and Legacy

  • February 9, 1989: Tezuka dies of stomach cancer in Tokyo.
  • Last Words: Requests to continue working, unwilling to part with drawing equipment.
  • Burial: Despite being agnostic, buried in a Buddhist cemetery.
  • Posthumous Discovery (2014): Daughter finds artifacts, including sketches and essays.

Tezuka Osamu's legacy as the "God of Manga" remains influential, with iconic creations like Astro Boy, Phoenix, and Black Jack continuing to impact the manga and anime industry to this day.
